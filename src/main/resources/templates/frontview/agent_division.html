Inspection

Monitoring the production process requires an on-the-spot presence to reduce the risk of substandard quality, and to ensure that all other aspects of contractual obligations are met. The earlier those possible execution shortcomings are detected the better, in order to initiate timely corrective action.

  • 2. Pre‑production inspection
  • 3. Inline inspection
  • 4. Final (pre‑shipment) inspection

Quality Standards

For all our inspections we use the internationally recognized Acceptable Quality Level (AQL) standards. For each Inspection, this allows the client to set their acceptable quality tolerance levels.

Acceptable Quality Level (AQL)

The Acceptable Quality Level, commonly referred to as AQL, is a method widely used to measure a production order sample to determine whether the order as a whole has met the clientï¿½s quality standards and specifications. The client then has the data to make an informed decision to accept or reject the consignment.
